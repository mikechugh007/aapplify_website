Who is AAPPLIFY for?

AAPPLIFY is built for security, identity, data, compliance, platform, release, and application teams that need focused dashboards while sharing the same evidence, policies, workflows, and controls.

What is the control plane?

It is the operating layer that turns identity, change, security, data, and policy context into governed action through consoles, capabilities, signals, orchestration, controls, and modules.

What does each department see?

Each department can set its own default console. Security may focus on exposure paths, identity on access drift, data on policy movement, and change teams on approvals and control impact.

What systems do you connect first?

Most pilots start with the systems behind one high-priority workflow: identity providers, change tools, security platforms, data systems, ticketing, cloud logs, and existing policy files.

How do signals work?

Signals filter raw events into risk intelligence. They decide what matters enough to alert, escalate, open a review, trigger workflow, or require a control.

What does orchestration do?

Orchestration turns signals into governed response through agents, workflows, approvals, reviews, remediation, escalations, and handoffs.

How are controls handled?

Controls map policies, guardrails, posture requirements, identity/change rules, approvals, and compliance evidence back to the source data and workflow decisions.

Can AAPPLIFY use existing policy files?

Yes. Modules can process policy files, procedures, runbooks, and control documents so teams can draft workflows, controls, and review logic from the policies they already use.

What access does AAPPLIFY need?

AAPPLIFY can start read-only with least-privilege access. Teams can add write actions, approvals, or enforcement only after the workflow and control model is trusted.

What is included in a pilot?

A pilot usually includes one department console, a small set of connected sources, initial signals, one or two governed workflows, and the controls or evidence needed to validate fit.
