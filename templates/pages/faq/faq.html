What is AI Agent Control Assurance?

AI Agent Control Assurance is the practice of proving that AI agents and autonomous workflows had the right owner, identity, access scope, policy decision, approval, remediation path, exception status, and audit-ready evidence before they touched enterprise systems.

Who is AAPPLIFY for?

AAPPLIFY is built for CISOs, security assurance, GRC, identity governance, platform security, cloud security, security operations, and risk leaders who need proof that AI-agent and high-risk enterprise actions were controlled.

How does this extend beyond AI agents?

It is the discipline of proving that risky access, data, cloud, change, third-party, and AI-agent actions had the right policy decision, owner, approval, remediation or exception, and audit-ready evidence.

What does each department see?

Each team sees the control work it owns. Security may focus on exposure remediation, identity on privileged access, data on owner approval, and platform teams on production change impact.

What systems do you connect first?

Most pilots start with the systems behind one high-priority workflow: identity providers, change tools, security platforms, data systems, ticketing, cloud logs, and existing policy files.

How do signals work?

Signals filter raw events into control decisions. They decide what matters enough to require approval, remediation, exception handling, escalation, or evidence.

What does orchestration do?

Workflow turns signals into owned action through approvals, reviews, remediation, exceptions, escalations, and handoffs.

How are controls handled?

Controls map policies, owners, approval requirements, remediation steps, exception rules, and evidence obligations back to source systems and workflow decisions.

Can AAPPLIFY use existing policy files?

Yes. Modules can process policy files, procedures, runbooks, and control documents so teams can draft workflows, controls, and review logic from the policies they already use.

What access does AAPPLIFY need?

AAPPLIFY can start read-only with least-privilege access. Teams can add write actions, approvals, or enforcement only after the workflow and control model is trusted.

What is included in a pilot?

A pilot usually includes one high-risk workflow, a small set of connected sources, initial risk signals, one or two approval or remediation paths, and the evidence needed to validate fit.
